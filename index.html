Robotics is BBC's most successful co-corricular activity at the College. BBC Robotics entered over 15 teams into RoboCup's State and National Compitions this year, every team that was entered placed 1st, 2nd or 3rd in States, Nationals or both events.
BBC Robotics comprises of 4 'stages' for Students to progress from having minimal knowledge about robotics to being some of the best in Australia.

Introductory

BBC's Robotics Program starts with Year 4 groups learning the basics of building and programming using LEGO EV3 Kits, they meet once a week with Lachlan Firth, an ex-robotics program member.

Entry to RoboCup

Year 5 & 6 Students are able to enter into RoboCup Dance with the ability to compete at Regionals, States and Nationals.

Rescue

Year 7-9 Students compete in Rescue. Robots must be able to follow a black line, detect colored squares and act accordingly, detect & avoid obstacles, and Find, Grab and remove a can from a green tile. Rescue is offered at Regionals, States and Nationals in 3 divisions; primary, secondary and open.

Senior - Soccer or Rescue

Year 10-12 students can choose to remain in Rescue or compete in Soccer. Soccer robots must play soccer with an infrared ball, detect outlines, use a compass to align the robot and even use a camera to track the back of the soccer goal. Each team must make 2 robots.
